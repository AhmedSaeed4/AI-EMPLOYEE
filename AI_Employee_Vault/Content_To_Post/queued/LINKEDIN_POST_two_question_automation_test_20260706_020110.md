# Stop asking "can AI do this?" — ask these 2 questions instead

Everyone's testing AI on random tasks. Most quit because the results feel... off.

The problem isn't the tool. It's the question. "Can AI do this?" almost always returns yes — and that yes pulls you into busywork, not leverage.

Try this instead. Before automating anything, ask:

**1. Is it repetitive and rule-based?**
Invoices, lead intake, follow-ups, report formatting, scheduling. If a task follows the same path every time, it belongs to a digital FTE — not a human. Every hour spent here is an hour stolen from work that actually grows the business.

**2. Does it need judgment in front of a customer?**
Negotiations, creative direction, hard conversations, the final "is this good?" call. AI can draft, summarize, and prep — but the human owns the last mile.

Most teams get this backwards. They try to automate the judgment work and drown in the repetitive work. Flip it and everything moves.

## Key Takeaways
- "Can AI do this?" is the wrong question — it almost always says yes
- Rule-based, repetitive work → automate it; it's a digital FTE's job
- Customer-facing judgment → keep human, let AI assist, never replace the last mile
