# I Did the Math. Hiring vs Building AI. The Numbers Changed Everything.

Let's be honest — most posts about AI talk about "potential" and "future." This one has actual numbers.

**Option A: Hire a junior assistant**

Salary: $35,000/year
Benefits + overhead: ~$12,000/year
Training time: 3-6 months before they're actually useful
Working hours: 8 hours/day, 5 days/week
Vacation/sick days: ~15 days/year they're NOT working

**Total cost: ~$47,000/year**
**Actual working hours: ~1,900 hours/year**

**Option B: Build a Digital FTE**

Setup cost: varies by complexity (think 1-2 months of that junior salary)
Operating cost: $200-500/month depending on what it handles
Training time: 2-3 weeks with continuous improvement
Working hours: 24 hours/day, 7 days/week
Zero vacation, zero sick days, zero turnover

**Total cost: ~$3,600-6,000/year**
**Actual working hours: 8,760 hours/year**

That's not a comparison. That's a different universe.

Now, I'm not saying fire everyone. Your best people do work AI can't — strategy, relationships, creativity, judgment.

But the tasks you're paying $47,000 for someone to copy-paste emails and schedule posts? Those should cost $3,600. And work 4x the hours.

## Key Takeaways
- Calculate your TRUE cost per hour worked (include benefits, overhead, non-working days)
- The ROI isn't just cost — it's having coverage nights, weekends, holidays
- Best approach: automate the repetitive, keep the humans for work that needs human judgment
