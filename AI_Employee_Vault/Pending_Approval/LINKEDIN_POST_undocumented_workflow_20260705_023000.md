# The #1 reason your AI project stalls has nothing to do with AI.

You bought the tool, wrote the prompt, the demo worked. Then it hit real work and quietly fell apart.

Here's the part nobody talks about: an AI agent can only automate a process that's been *captured* — written down, step by step, with the branch points and the exceptions.

And in most businesses, the real workflow — the one that actually runs the company — lives in one person's head. Usually the founder's. Never in a doc.

So the AI automates the *described* process, not the *actual* one. The gap between those two is where every "it almost worked" lives.

Three places your real workflow hides:

**1. In someone's head**
The steps nobody questions because "that's just how we do it." They break the moment that person is sick, on leave, or tries to hand off.

**2. In chat threads**
Decisions made in Slack and email that never reach a system. The institutional memory of "we tried that, here's why we stopped."

**3. In the exceptions**
The refunds, the VIP clients, the edge cases — the 20% that don't follow the happy path. If your AI doesn't know these exist, it'll fumble them publicly.

Before you build a single agent, the highest-leverage hour you can spend is writing down one real workflow, messiness included. That doc is what your AI employee actually runs on.

What's one process in your business that lives entirely in someone's head? 👇
