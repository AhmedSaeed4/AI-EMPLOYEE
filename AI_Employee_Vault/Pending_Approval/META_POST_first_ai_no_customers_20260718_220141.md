# Your first AI employee should never talk to a customer. 🤐

Everyone wants their first Digital FTE to do the exciting stuff — answer new leads, post content, reply to comments, run the socials.

That's also the fastest way to lose a deal and trash your reputation in one weekend.

Here's the reframe nobody gives you: customer-facing work is the *highest-risk* place to start an AI employee. A clumsy reply to a hot lead costs you a client. A botched social post costs you trust you spent years building. And you'll have almost no reps to learn from, because each mistake is loud and expensive.

The smarter starting point is the invisible, boring, high-volume back-office work — lead routing, data entry, invoice chasing, report generation, inbox triage. 🗂️

Why? Because that's where mistakes are cheap and the reps are endless. Your AI employee gets thousands of low-stakes attempts to become reliable *before* it ever faces a customer. By the time it does, it's battle-tested — not a live experiment on your best leads.

The trust ramp matters: **invisible work → low-touch work → customer-facing.** 🔄 Skip the first steps and you don't get the compound benefit. You get a flashy demo that breaks the moment a real person asks an unexpected question.

Build the boring one first. Let it earn your trust in the dark, then promote it.

## Key Takeaways
- 🎯 Start your AI employee on invisible back-office work, not customers
- 📈 High volume + low risk = the fastest path to a reliable Digital FTE
- 🪜 Trust ramps in stages — back-office first, customer-facing last
