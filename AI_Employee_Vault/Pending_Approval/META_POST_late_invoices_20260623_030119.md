# You finished the work. Your money is still sitting in a draft folder. 📁

There's a folder in your business quietly holding thousands of dollars hostage — and it's not your expenses. It's your **"Draft Invoice"** folder.

Here's the pattern nobody talks about:

- You deliver the project ✅
- You tell yourself "I'll send the invoice tonight" 🌙
- Tonight becomes tomorrow. Tomorrow becomes next week.
- The client, who was thrilled on Monday, has already forgotten by Friday.

Late invoicing is the silent cash-flow killer. And it bleeds you in two directions at once:

**Direction 1:** You delay *sending* it — so the payment clock starts late.
**Direction 2:** You send it once, then never *chase* it — because chasing feels awkward, and awkward tasks always get pushed to "later."

Every day an invoice sits unpaid, you're giving your client an interest-free loan with YOUR money. And the harsher truth: the longer a bill goes uncollected, the lower the odds it ever gets paid in full. 📉

The fix isn't more discipline. Discipline is exactly what already failed you. The fix is a system that removes the human hesitation:

✅ Invoice drafted the moment work is marked complete
⏰ Gentle reminder on day 7, day 14, day 21 — automatically
🔔 Overdue ones flagged to YOU, for the one conversation that genuinely needs a human touch

That's a Digital FTE doing the part you hate (the asking) so you're never the awkward one — and your cash keeps moving instead of evaporating.

## Key Takeaways
- Late invoicing isn't a money problem — it's a "who's doing the asking" problem
- Every unpaid day is an interest-free loan to your client
- Automate the asking, keep the human part for the exceptions
