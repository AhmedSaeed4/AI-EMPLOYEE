# Everyone sells speed. Here's what "24-hour delivery" actually costs you.

Every AI agency and animation studio now opens with the same line: "done in 24 hours."

It sounds great in a sales call. It's quietly expensive in practice.

Here's what usually gets sacrificed to hit that number:

- **Strategy** — your project gets a template, not a solution built around your business
- **Iteration** — you get version one. No rounds, no refinement, no polish
- **Quality** — the last 10% (the part that makes work feel premium) is the first thing cut

Speed isn't free. It's paid for in generic output and rework you'll end up doing yourself.

That's why my timelines range from 24 hours to 30 days — based on scope, not a marketing claim. A quick automation? Fast. A Digital FTE that touches real revenue? That deserves room to breathe.

I'd rather tell you two weeks and overdeliver than promise 24 hours and hand you something forgettable.

## Key Takeaways
- "Fast" is usually a template wearing a disguise
- Quality lives in the last 10% — the part shortcuts skip
- Honest timelines scale with scope, not sales pressure
