# Stop calling them "quick tasks." They're quietly bankrupting your business. 🪤

"You got 5 minutes?"

That's how it starts. A tiny favor. A quick check. A 2-minute reply.

But a "quick task" is never 2 minutes. By the time you context-switch in, handle it, and switch back — it's 20. Do that 30 times a day and you've crossed off a mountain of small things… and finished zero real work. 😮‍💨

The expensive part isn't the time. It's the **attention**. Every quick task fragments your focus, drains your energy, and quietly shoves your actual work into the evening — and your evenings into the weekend.

Here's the shift that changes everything: quick tasks don't need *you*. They need *someone*. A digital employee that absorbs the small stuff — replies, follow-ups, reminders, sorting, summaries — so you stay in deep work where your real value lives. 🤝

Save the "quick things" for the system. Save yourself for the work only you can do. 🚀

## Key Takeaways
- A "quick task" costs 5–10x its face value once you factor in context-switching
- The real damage is to your attention and energy, not your clock
- Automate the small stuff → protect the deep work that actually grows the business
