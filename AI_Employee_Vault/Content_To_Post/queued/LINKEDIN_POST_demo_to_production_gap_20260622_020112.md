# Your AI Worked Perfectly in the Demo. Then Nobody Touched It Again.

A founder shows me their new AI setup. The demo is flawless — it drafts emails, summarizes docs, answers questions. Everyone nods.

Two weeks later, the login count is zero.

This is the most expensive moment in AI adoption, and almost nobody talks about it. It's called the demo-to-production gap, and most estimates put 70–80% of AI projects on the wrong side of it.

The demo wasn't wrong. The deployment was missing.

Here's what almost always fails:

1. **It lives in a separate tab.** If your AI isn't where the work already happens, it becomes friction — and friction loses to habit every time.
2. **Nobody owns it.** A tool without an owner is a tool that quietly dies. Someone has to feed it, check it, and trust it.
3. **It was built to impress, not to run.** A five-minute demo can't survive a Monday morning. Production is a different craft.

A real digital FTE isn't a demo you show off. It's a system that runs while you sleep — on the boring, messy, repeatable work that actually fills your week.

The question was never "can AI do this?" It's "will it still be doing it in 90 days?"

## Key Takeaways
- The gap between a great demo and a daily-used system is where most AI projects die
- Tools fail when they live apart from the work or lack an owner
- A digital FTE earns its keep by running in production, not in a showcase
