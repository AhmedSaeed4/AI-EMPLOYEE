# I give my AI employees a performance review. Here's the scorecard.

Most people build an AI employee, switch it on, and never look at it again. Six months later they can't tell you whether it's actually helping or just "running."

You'd never do that with a human hire. So why do it with a digital one?

Every Friday I run a five-minute review on each AI employee using four numbers:

**1. Hours reclaimed**
The blunt one. How many hours of human work did it actually absorb this week? If you can't point to a number, you don't have an employee — you have a vibe.

**2. Clean completion rate**
What % of tasks did it finish without a human having to rescue it? When this drops, the task scope is drifting and needs tightening.

**3. Escalation accuracy**
When it handed work to a human, was it the right human, at the right moment? A bad handoff is worse than no automation — it just adds a step.

**4. Break-and-recover time**
When it failed, how long until it was back? An employee that crashes and stays down isn't an employee.

You don't need a fancy dashboard. You need four numbers and five honest minutes a week. The AI employees that get reviewed are the ones that compound. The ones that don't quietly stop earning their keep.

If you had an AI employee right now, which of these four numbers could you not answer? Drop it below 👇
