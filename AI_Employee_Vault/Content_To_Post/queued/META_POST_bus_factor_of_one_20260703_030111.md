# The most dangerous sentence in any small business: "Only [name] knows how to do that." 🚨

Every business has one.

The person who holds the passwords, the client relationships, the pricing, the "way we've always done it." They're invaluable — and that's exactly the problem.

Because when a process lives in a person's head instead of a system, that person becomes a single point of failure. They go on vacation, the work stalls. They get sick, invoices sit unopened. They give two weeks' notice, and the business quietly restarts from zero. 📉

The fix isn't hiring more people. It's moving knowledge out of heads and into systems — documented, repeatable, and ready to run without anyone's permission. That's exactly where a Digital FTE earns its keep: it doesn't just do the task, it *holds* the task. It becomes the reliable pair of hands that keeps the wheels turning when the one person who "knows how" is suddenly out.

A healthy business should pass the bus test: if your key person didn't show up Monday, would it still run? 🚌

## Key Takeaways
- A process that only exists in one person's head is a liability, not an asset
- The goal isn't more headcount — it's codified, repeatable systems
- A Digital FTE turns tribal knowledge into infrastructure that keeps working 24/7
