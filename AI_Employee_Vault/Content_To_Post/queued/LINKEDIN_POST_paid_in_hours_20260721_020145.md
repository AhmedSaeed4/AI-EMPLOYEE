Every Monday you reformat that report by hand. Every Friday you chase the same three invoices. Every new lead gets a reply you type from scratch, hours after they expected it.

That's not "just part of running a business." That's an AI employee's workload — and right now you're the one doing it, unpaid, on top of the work you actually get paid for.

If your hour is worth $100 to your business, and you spend 8 hours a week on tasks a Digital FTE could handle — that's $800 a week. Over $40,000 a year. And that's before counting the leads you lost because the follow-up went out too late.

So the question was never "can I afford to automate?"

It's "can I keep affording to do it myself?"

The cost of a Digital FTE isn't measured against zero. It's measured against the hours you're already burning — and the higher-value work those hours are stealing from.
