# Your business already has an org chart full of jobs nobody was hired for.

You just don't see it — because you're the one filling every seat.

The newsletter you "sometimes" send? That's a Head of Content.
The lead that waits two days for a reply? That's a Sales Development Rep.
The invoice you chase manually at month-end? That's an Accounts person.
The reel you meant to post last week? That's a Social Media Manager.

These roles already exist in your business. They're just staffed by a tired version of you, after hours, doing the work inconsistently — which means most of it quietly doesn't get done at all.

Here's the reframe that changes everything: an AI employee doesn't create new work. It officially fills a role that's currently running on your spare time and goodwill — and doing it better than you do at 9pm on a Sunday.

You're not automating tasks. You're finally hiring for the jobs you've been silently doing for free.

The businesses scaling without burning out aren't superhuman. They just wrote down their invisible org chart — and staffed it with something that never clocks out.

- Every recurring task is a role — usually one you're filling yourself, unpaid, after hours
- A Digital FTE doesn't create work; it officially staffs roles already running on your time
