# Your client isn't paying you for the work. They're paying you for the work *around* the work.

Think about the last project you delivered.

The actual skill — the build, the animation, the strategy — was maybe 30% of why they felt looked after.

The other 70%? The reply that came in an hour, not three days. The "on track for Thursday" message. The follow-up that arrived before they had to chase. The clean proposal.

None of it is your craft. All of it is why they refer you.

Here's the part most owners miss: that 70% is also the easiest to hand off — and the most expensive to keep. It's where your hours leak. It's the first thing to slip when you're busy. And it's exactly what makes a client think "they don't have capacity for me" right before they go quiet.

A Digital FTE doesn't do your craft. It carries the work between the work — the replies, confirmations, and follow-ups in your tone, inside guardrails you set once — so the part only you can do gets your full attention.

- Clients experience your coordination as much as your craft — the "work around the work" is often why they trust and refer you
- That same 70% is the most expensive to do yourself: it leaks hours, slips when you're busy, and signals low capacity to clients
- A Digital FTE carries the repeatable coordination layer so your actual craft finally gets your full attention
