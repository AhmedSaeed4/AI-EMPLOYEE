# Your Content Looks Like Everyone Else's. That's Why It's Dying.

Pull up your feed right now.

Same stock photo. Same carousel template. Same "5 tips" headline on the same gradient background. Every brand slowly converging on the exact same look — then wondering why reach keeps dropping.

Here's the part nobody says out loud: **the algorithm isn't killing your content. Sameness is.**

When ten businesses post the same template, the platform has nothing to rank. You've made yourself interchangeable — and interchangeable content earns interchangeable results.

The brands getting saved, shared, and DMed right now aren't posting *more*. They're posting something unmistakable:

- Custom motion you can't screenshot off a template site
- A visual style people recognize in the first two seconds
- Reels that feel like your brand, not like a stock library

This is the whole reason custom 3D and visual work exists — not to look "cool," but to give your business a face that can't be copied.

Anyone can post every day. Almost no one posts something that's unmistakably theirs.

## Key Takeaways
- Reach drops when content becomes interchangeable — not when the algorithm turns on you
- Distinct visuals (custom motion, a recognizable style) are what make a brand un-copyable
- More volume won't fix a sameness problem. Only a visual identity will.
