# The Real Math Behind Automation ROI (Most Businesses Get This Wrong)

Here's a number that should make you uncomfortable:

The average small business owner spends 40% of their day on tasks that have nothing to do with their actual product or service.

At a $100/hour effective rate, that's $160/day. $800/week. **$41,600/year** — spent on work a machine could do.

And no, I'm not talking about replacing anyone. I'm talking about reclaiming YOUR time.

Here's the math framework I walk clients through:

**Step 1: List your non-core tasks**
Email management, scheduling, data entry, invoicing, follow-ups, reporting.

**Step 2: Estimate hours per week**
Most people underestimate. Track it for 3 days — you'll be shocked.

**Step 3: Multiply by your hourly value**
Not your salary. Your VALUE. What's one hour of your strategic thinking worth to the business?

**Step 4: Compare to automation costs**
Most AI automation runs $200-500/month for the tasks listed above. That's a 10-20x return when done right.

One client did this exercise and discovered they were spending $3,200/month worth of their time on tasks costing $300/month to automate.

The math isn't complicated. The hard part is sitting down to actually do it.

## Key Takeaways
- Your time has a hidden price tag — calculate it
- Track non-core tasks for 3 days before dismissing automation
- The ROI isn't theoretical — it's basic arithmetic
