# You don't need an AI strategy. You need a "never again" list.

Almost every business I talk to wants to start with a grand plan — "our AI strategy," "our automation roadmap." And almost every time, that's exactly where it stalls.

Strategy decks don't automate anything.

Here's what actually works.

Open a note on your phone. For one week, every time a task makes you sigh — the report you reformat every Monday, the invoice you chase by hand, the caption you rewrite for the tenth time — write it down. One line. That's a "never again."

By Friday you won't have a strategy. You'll have something better: a ranked list of tasks you already hate doing, that already eat your hours, and that are perfect candidates for an AI employee to take over.

The businesses that build Digital FTEs fastest don't start with vision. They start with friction. They automate the sigh first — and that one win funds the next.

Your AI strategy is hiding in the tasks you keep promising yourself you'll "never do again." Go find them.

## Key Takeaways
- Strategy decks stall; a "never again" list moves — it captures real, repeating friction
- For one week, log every task that makes you sigh — you'll surface your best automation candidates
- The fastest AI wins come from automating friction first, not from building a perfect roadmap

What's the one task you swore you'd "never do manually again" — and still did this week? Drop it below, and I'll tell you whether it's worth automating first.
