# You wouldn't onboard a new hire with one sentence. So why are you deploying your AI employee that way? 🤔

Most people get a new AI tool, paste in a prompt, and declare it "live." Then three weeks later they're convinced AI just "doesn't work for their business."

It's not that AI doesn't work. It's that they onboarded a full-time employee like it was a browser extension. 🧩

A real Digital FTE — one that actually takes work off your plate — needs the same things a human hire does:

1. **A clear job description.** Not "help with admin." A specific outcome: "respond to every new lead within 5 minutes, Mon–Fri."
2. **Documented steps.** The exact way *you* do it today, written down once. This is also where you discover your process was never as clear as you thought. 📝
3. **A boss and a review cadence.** Someone checks its work daily at first, then weekly. No supervision = drift.
4. **Clear boundaries.** What it can decide alone, and what it must escalate to a human. Money, public replies, deletions — those stay with you.

Skip any one of these and you don't have an employee. You have a very expensive guessing machine. 💸

The businesses seeing real returns aren't using smarter AI. They're onboarding it like it's the most important hire of the year — because right now, it often is.

## Key Takeaways
- An AI employee fails the same way a human hire fails: unclear role, no docs, no oversight
- Documenting your process for an AI pays you back twice — once for the AI, once for your own team
- The winners aren't buying better tech, they're treating deployment like onboarding, not installation
