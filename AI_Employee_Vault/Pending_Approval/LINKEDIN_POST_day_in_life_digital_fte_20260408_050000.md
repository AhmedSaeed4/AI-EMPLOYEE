# What My AI Employee Actually Does All Day

I keep seeing posts about why businesses "should" use AI.

So let me show you what it actually looks like in practice.

**6:00 AM** — My Digital FTE checks the inbox. Flags 3 urgent emails. Drafts responses for the routine ones. Logs everything.

**9:00 AM** — While I'm having my first coffee, it's already posted today's social content across LinkedIn, Instagram, and Twitter. Content it wrote, scheduled, and queued — all approved by me the night before.

**12:00 PM** — A lead fills out a contact form. Within 60 seconds, they get a personalized follow-up. Not a generic template. A response based on what they actually asked about.

**5:00 PM** — I get a daily briefing: what happened, what needs my attention, what got done. Everything logged. Nothing forgotten.

**Midnight** — It's still working. Monitoring. Organizing. Preparing tomorrow's tasks.

The total cost of this "employee"? A fraction of what I'd pay a part-time assistant.

The ROI? My human team now spends 90% of their time on work that actually requires human judgment.

## Key Takeaways
- AI employees don't replace your team — they handle the 60% of work that doesn't need a human
- The magic is in the system: perception (watches for triggers) → reasoning (decides what to do) → action (does it)
- Start with one workflow. Inbox triage is usually the easiest win
