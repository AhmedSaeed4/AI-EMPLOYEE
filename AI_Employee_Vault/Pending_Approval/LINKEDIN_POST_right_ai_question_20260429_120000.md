# Stop asking "What can AI do?" Start asking THIS instead.

Most business owners I talk to are asking the wrong question about AI.

They come to me with: "What can AI do for my business?"

And honestly? That question leads to shiny object syndrome. You start exploring every tool, every chatbot, every trend — and end up with a dozen subscriptions and zero results.

The better question is: **"What is my team doing right now that a machine should be doing?"**

That shift changes everything.

Instead of chasing capabilities, you start spotting inefficiencies. You stop thinking about AI as a buzzword and start seeing it as a workforce multiplier.

Here's a quick test:
- If a task is repetitive, it's a candidate.
- If a task requires zero creativity, it's a candidate.
- If a task takes more than 15 minutes and happens daily, it's a candidate.

Most businesses I work with find 5-10 hours of recoverable time in the first week of asking the right question.

That's the difference between an AI strategy that collects dust and one that actually moves revenue.

What's one task your team repeats every day that you'd love to hand off? Drop it below — I bet it's more automatable than you think.
