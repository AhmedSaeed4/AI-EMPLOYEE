# You don't have an AI problem. You have a delegation problem. 🎯

You bought the tools. You watched the demos. You even tried a few prompts. 🤖

But here's the uncomfortable truth most businesses won't admit: your AI isn't underperforming — your instructions are.

When you onboard a human teammate, you give them a clear role, a set of inputs, a definition of "done," and a rule for when to ask versus when to act. Yet with AI, most people type one vague sentence and expect magic. Then they declare, "AI just doesn't work for my business." 😬

The shift happens the moment you stop treating AI like a search box and start treating it like a new hire with a job description. Give it a clear role. Define the inputs. Set the acceptance criteria. Build escalation rules for the edge cases. Do that, and it stops being a toy and starts acting like headcount that never sleeps. 🚀

That's exactly what we build — Digital FTEs engineered to own a job end-to-end, not just answer questions.

## Key Takeaways
- AI usually fails when it's briefed like a search box, not a teammate
- Every role needs four things: a clear job, defined inputs, acceptance criteria, and escalation rules
- The right brief turns a clever tool into reliable headcount that works 24/7
