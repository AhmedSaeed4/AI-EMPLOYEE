# Most of what's burning you out isn't work. It's decisions.

Every day you make dozens of micro-decisions no one ever sees: which lead to answer first, what the follow-up should say, whether that invoice looks right, which version of the caption to ship.

None of it feels like a task. None of it lands on your to-do list. But by 3pm your brain is fried — and you can't explain why, because on paper you "didn't do much."

Here's the reframe. What drains you isn't the doing. It's the deciding. And deciding is the one thing everyone tries to automate last, when it should be first.

A Digital FTE doesn't just run tasks. It makes the routine decisions for you, inside guardrails you set once — "reply to leads within an hour, in this tone, never commit to a date." Suddenly you're not the bottleneck on a hundred small daily calls.

The real unlock isn't delegating your tasks. It's delegating your decisions — the ones that never actually needed you.

## Key Takeaways
- The hidden drain on your day isn't task volume — it's decision load
- Most "busywork" is low-stakes decisions that need a human only because no rules exist yet
- A Digital FTE removes decision load by making routine calls inside guardrails you define once
