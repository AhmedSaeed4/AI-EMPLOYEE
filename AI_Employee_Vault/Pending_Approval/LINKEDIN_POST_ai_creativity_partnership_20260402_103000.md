# The Best Creative Work I've Seen Lately? Human + AI, Not Human vs AI

There's a misconception that AI kills creativity.

The reality? The best results I see come from creatives who treat AI as a collaborator, not a replacement.

**Here's what that looks like in practice:**

A client came to us needing a series of animated social videos. Traditional timeline: 2-3 weeks.

Using AI-assisted workflows:
- Concept and storyboarding: Human creativity
- Initial motion tests: AI-generated variations
- Refinement and polish: Human eye for detail
- Final delivery: 4 days

The AI didn't replace the creative decisions. It removed the tedious iteration cycles.

**The creative still:**
- Directed the vision
- Made the aesthetic choices
- Ensured brand consistency
- Added the human touch that makes content connect

**The AI just:**
- Generated options faster
- Handled repetitive tasks
- Freed up creative time for... actual creativity

This isn't about doing less work. It's about doing more of the work that matters.

## Key Takeaways
- AI handles the tedious parts, you handle the creative parts
- Speed comes from removing friction, not cutting corners
- The best output still needs human judgment and taste

How are you using AI in your creative workflow?
