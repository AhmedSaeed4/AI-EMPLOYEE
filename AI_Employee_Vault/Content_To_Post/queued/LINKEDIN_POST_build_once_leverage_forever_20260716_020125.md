# The most expensive thing in your business is something you only make once.

A one-off is the most expensive unit of work there is — because it amortizes over exactly one use.

Think about it:
- The pitch deck you rebuild every time
- The report you format from scratch each Monday
- The reel you pour hours into, post once, and let die
- The follow-up you type by hand, again and again

Every one of those carries a hidden cost: you'll pay it again next week. And the week after.

Here's the reframe. The businesses with the best margins don't work harder than you. They've stopped making one-offs and started making assets — work that pays you back long after you build it once.

A content template that spins out a hundred variations. A Digital FTE that runs the same report at 6am every day without you. One animation system that produces a fresh cut every week instead of a single hero video that fades by Friday.

The shift isn't "do more." It's "stop rebuilding what you've already built."

## Key Takeaways
- One-off work is the most expensive kind — it amortizes over a single use
- High-margin businesses convert repeat work into assets: templates, systems, agents
- You don't need more output. You need each piece of work to pay you back more than once
