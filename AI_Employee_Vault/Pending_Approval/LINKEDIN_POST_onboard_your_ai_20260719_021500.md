# You spend two weeks onboarding a new hire. You gave your AI two minutes — then called it "useless."

Think about how you bring a human employee up to speed. You hand them your SOPs. You sit with them for a week. You correct the tone of their first emails. You give them a "here's how we do things" doc and a list of mistakes to avoid.

You invest in onboarding because you know an untrained hire costs you more than they save.

Now think about how you "trained" your AI.

Most businesses open a chat window, paste one prompt, and expect a tool that has never seen their business to instantly write like them, decide like them, and never make a mistake.

When it doesn't — generic tone, wrong answers, off-brand output — they blame the AI. "It just doesn't get it."

Here's the reframe: a Digital FTE isn't a tool you install. It's an employee you onboard.

The difference between a ChatGPT tab that frustrates you and an AI employee that runs 24/7 is onboarding — your SOPs, your brand voice, your decision rules, your "never do this" list, the context a human hire would get in week one.

This is the first thing I build for clients. Not the automation. The onboarding. Because an AI employee that knows your business outperforms any tool that's guessing.

What's the one task you'd hand to an AI employee tomorrow — if it already understood your business? Tell me below 👇

## Key Takeaways
- Untrained AI produces generic output because it has none of your business context
- A Digital FTE works like a new hire: it needs onboarding, SOPs, and brand guardrails to perform
- The gap between "AI doesn't work" and "AI runs my operations 24/7" is almost always onboarding
