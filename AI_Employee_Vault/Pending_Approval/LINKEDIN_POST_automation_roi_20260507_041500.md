# Stop Paying for Busywork — Here's the Math Your Competitors Already Know

I ran the numbers on repetitive tasks — email triage, invoice chasing, lead follow-up — and the average founder burns 15-20 hours per week on work a machine could handle.

That's half a work week. Gone.

Here's what most businesses get wrong about automation:

They wait until they're drowning before they start. By then, the bottleneck is compounded — missed follow-ups, delayed invoices, stale leads. The cost isn't just time. It's revenue leaking out the side door every single day.

The fix isn't hiring more people. It's building a system that catches the small stuff so your team can focus on the big stuff.

## Key Takeaways
- The average founder spends 15-20 hrs/week on tasks that can be automated
- Waiting to automate compounds the cost — every missed follow-up is lost revenue
- Start with the smallest, most repetitive task — the compounding time savings are massive
- Automation isn't about replacing people — it's about freeing them for high-value work
