# You're Not Losing Leads to Your Competitors. You're Losing Them to Silence.

A lead says "not right now." You move on. Three months later, you spend money acquiring a brand-new lead — while the one you already paid for quietly buys from someone who simply kept showing up.

Here's the math nobody runs: research consistently shows most sales need 5–12 touches to close. Yet the average team gives up after one or two.

So that deal wasn't lost to a competitor. It was lost in the gap between touch #2 and touch #5 — where nobody followed up.

This is where every human pipeline breaks. Follow-up is the most valuable work you do — and the least urgent. So it's the first thing a busy week quietly kills.

A digital FTE doesn't forget. It runs the nurture cadence you can't sustain: the check-in at week 3, the useful nudge at week 6, the "still thinking about it?" at month 2. Every lead, every time, on time.

Your most profitable channel isn't more ads. It's the leads you already paid to acquire.

## Key Takeaways
- Most sales close after 5+ touches — but human teams stop at one or two
- Follow-up is high-value, low-urgency work, so it dies first in a busy week
- A digital FTE runs the nurture humans skip, turning paid-for leads into revenue
