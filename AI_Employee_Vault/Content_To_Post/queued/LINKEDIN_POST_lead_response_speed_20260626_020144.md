# The Deal Wasn't Lost in the Pitch. It Was Lost in the First 5 Minutes.

A lead fills out your form at 2:14pm. You see it at 4:30. You reply at 5:10.

By then, they've already messaged two competitors, read three reviews, and started comparing quotes.

Here's the part most businesses miss: the research on inbound leads is brutally consistent. The odds of converting drop sharply in the minutes after an inquiry — not after the demo, not after the price. In the gap between their interest and your reply.

Speed isn't a tactic. It's the moat.

And it's the one thing a human team almost never sustains. Lunch breaks. Off days. The meeting that ran long. One missed ping and the lead quietly assumes you're slow — which, fairly or not, becomes their entire picture of what it's like to work with you.

This is exactly where a digital FTE changes the math. Not by replacing the relationship — by owning the gap. An instant first reply, a qualifier question, a booked slot — before a competitor has even opened the lead.

Your first impression isn't your website. It's your response time.

## Key Takeaways
- Conversion is decided in the minutes after a lead arrives, not in the pitch
- Human teams can't sustain instant replies — and one slow response reshapes the whole impression
- A digital FTE owns the gap so no inbound lead ever waits
