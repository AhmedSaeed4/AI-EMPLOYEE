# Before you build an AI agent, answer these 3 questions.

Everyone's building agents right now. Most of them never make it past a demo.

The difference between an AI employee that quietly saves you 10+ hours a week and a flashy tool that gathers dust isn't the technology. It's whether you asked the boring questions *before* the build.

Three I run with every project:

**1. What exact task is this replacing?**
Not "customer service." That's a department, not a task. A task is "answering the top 15 questions new leads ask, then handing the rest to a human." If you can't write the task on one line, your agent can't do it either.

**2. What does "done right" look like?**
Your agent needs a finish line, not a vibe. "Reply within 2 minutes, include the pricing link, flag anything about refunds for a human." Clear output, clear handoff. Vague goals build vague agents.

**3. Where does a human stay in the loop?**
The question nobody wants to ask — and the one that protects you. Payments, sensitive conversations, final approvals: decide upfront what the agent *suggests* and what it never decides alone.

Skip these and you get a cool demo. Answer them and you get an employee.

Building your first agent? Comment the task you're automating — I'll tell you if it's a fit or a trap.
